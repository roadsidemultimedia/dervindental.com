Kansas City area Dentist Dr. Dervin: Metal-Free Fillings

With regard to restorative substances that we place inside your mouth, Kansas City area dental work has improved to the point where silver mercury offers no real advantages over non-metal acrylics like ceramic and porcelain, which blend in with the color of your teeth.

Non-metal fillings outperform older materials in several important ways:

  • Unrivaled beauty — they can match existing tooth colors
  • Decrease further decay — with the Kansas City area’s dental work with powerful sealing techniques, new materials maximize your protection against future decay
  • No reactions with temperature — unlike metal, acrylics won’t expand or contract as temperatures change inside your mouth

For superior quality, optimum durability and prolonged beauty, porcelain and ceramic are rivaled. If you have older metal fillings, we can discuss how you and your mouth will thrive by replacing them with strong and beautiful non-metal fillings.

Metal-Free, Hassle-Free

Silver mercury fillings were invented at a time when the prevailing dental concept was to make a hole in the tooth, remove the decay and pack metal inside. Dentists relied on the expansion and contraction caused by metal’s micro-corrosive abilities to lock the filling in place.

Kansas City area dentist Richard Dervin explains, “Since the materials we have now are so much more advanced, there’s just no good reason to use amalgam. It is obsolete, like sending your child to school with an abacus instead of a calculator. There’s simply no way to justify it.”

In contrast, our advanced adhesive dental techniques allow our dentist in Shawnee, KS to bond your restoration to your tooth, helping to seal out decay and maximize protection. Compared to mercury, a known biohazard, our new materials are completely biocompatible and cause no adverse reactions. This means you keep much more of your own tooth structure in the long run.

Different inert non-metals are ideal for differing dental issues. We may use laboratory fabricated ceramics for inlays and onlays, Zirconia® for durable function, Empress® for sustained beauty — or a combination of several materials for more complex cases.
